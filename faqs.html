Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, including your basal metabolic rate and activity level.

How do I calculate TDEE?

You can calculate TDEE by using your age, gender, height, weight, and activity level. Our TDEE Calculator does this automatically for you.

What is BMR and how is it different from TDEE?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at rest. TDEE includes BMR plus calories burned through daily activities and exercise.

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?

To lose weight, you should create a calorie deficit. Typically, consuming 500 calories less than your TDEE per day helps achieve safe and sustainable fat loss.

Can TDEE help me gain muscle?

Yes! Eating more calories than your TDEE along with strength training can support muscle gain. Our calculator provides estimated calories for gain, maintenance, and fat loss.

Is the TDEE calculator accurate?

It provides an estimate based on scientific formulas. Individual results may vary depending on metabolism, lifestyle, and accuracy of the input data.

Can I use the TDEE calculator if I am very active?

Yes! You can select your correct activity level (e.g., lightly active, moderately active, very active, extra active) to get a more accurate estimate of your daily calorie needs.
